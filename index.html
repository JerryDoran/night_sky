Aquila

Brightest start is Altaire

Aquila is a constellation on the celestial equator. Its name is Latin for 'eagle' and it represents the bird that carried Zeus/Jupiter's thunderbolts.

Cygnus

Brightest start is Deneb

Cygnus is a northern constellation lying on the plane of the Milky Way, deriving its name from the Latinized Greek word for swan.

Read More

Draco

Brightest start is Gamma Draconis

Draco is a constellation in the far northern sky. Its name is Latin for dragon. It was one of the 48 constellations listed by the 2nd century astronomer Ptolemy.

Hercules

Brightest start is β Her (Kornephoros)

Hercules is a constellation named after Hercules, the Roman mythological hero adapted from the Greek hero Heracles.

Lyra

Brightest start is Vega

Lyra is a small constellation. It is one of 48 listed by the 2nd century astronomer Ptolemy, and is one of the 88 constellations recognized by the International Astronomical Union.

Read More

Sagitta

Brightest start is γ Sge

Sagitta is a dim but distinctive constellation in the northern sky. Its name is Latin for "arrow", not be confused with the significantly larger constellation Sagittarius.
