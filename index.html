explore asteroids

Asteroids are rocky remnants left over from the early formation of our solar system about 4.6 billion years ago. The current known asteroid count is a little over 1 million and growing. Most of these asteroids in our solar system can be found orbiting the Sun between Mars and Jupiter known as the asteroid belt. Asteroids range in size from about 300 miles in diameter — to bodies that are less than 50 feet across. The total mass of all the asteroids combined would make up less than the mass of Earth's moon
